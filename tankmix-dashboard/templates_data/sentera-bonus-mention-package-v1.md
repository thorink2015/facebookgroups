**Scout Before You Spray**

Every operator I talk to has the same blind spot. You fly the spray drone fine, but you don't know where the actual problems sit before you tank up. So you broadcast across the whole field and hope you caught it.

Sentera (the precision-ag arm under John Deere) has been quietly building the answer. Their 6X multispectral sensor mounts on a regular scout drone. You fly the field in about 20 minutes, FieldAgent kicks out a variable-rate prescription, and you load that prescription straight into your Agras for the spray pass.

What it actually does in the field:
- You stop broadcasting fungicide across healthy corn
- You hit only the disease pressure zones
- Your chemical bill drops on a typical pass
- The prescription file is your defensible record if drift or rate questions come up

I've watched a few of these workflows run alongside straight broadcast spraying. The math holds up.

If you fly an Agras and you've been thinking about scout-first, Sentera's worth a look: [sentera.com](https://sentera.com).
