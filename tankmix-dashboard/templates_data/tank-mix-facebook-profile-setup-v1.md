# Tank Mix Facebook Profile Setup v1

Your personal profile is the conversion engine. Every helpful comment you leave in 50 groups sends a curious operator or farmer to click your name. This is what they need to see.

---

## 1. Name field
Set your display name to: **Eugen [last name] · Tank Mix**

The middle dot (·) is allowed by Facebook and makes Tank Mix instantly visible in search results and comment author lines. Avoid full caps. Don't use "TM Newsletter" or anything that reads like a brand account, Facebook flags those on personal profiles.

## 2. Profile photo
Specs: 320x320 minimum, headshot, friendly not corporate.

What to shoot:
- Outdoors, ideally with a hint of crop field or open ground behind you (golden hour helps)
- Head and shoulders, looking at camera, light smile
- One color in your shirt that's NOT business-casual blue (a green polo, a worn cap, anything that reads "I'm in the country")
- Take 20 photos, pick the one where you look like the guy a farmer would buy a beer for

Avoid: drone photos of you holding a drone (looks staged), suit-and-tie shots (looks like a marketer), illustrated avatars (looks like a bot).

## 3. Cover photo
Specs: 851x315 pixels (desktop), but Facebook crops the center 640x360 on mobile so put critical content there.

Option A (recommended): a drone over a corn or wheat field at golden hour with a simple text overlay. Use your existing Spray Drone Society image prompt pack to generate or shoot it. Text overlay reads:

> Tank Mix · The free weekly for US ag drone spray operators

That's it. No other copy.

Option B (backup): a clean composite of 3 Tank Mix newsletter screenshots side by side. Same text overlay.

Skip: stock photos of generic farmland, busy collages, promotional graphics with multiple CTAs.

## 4. Intro / bio line (max 101 characters)
> "I write Tank Mix, the free weekly for US ag drone spray operators and farmer-operators."

If you want a shorter version that fits old Facebook UIs (under 80 chars):
> "Founder of Tank Mix · Free weekly for US ag drone operators · tankmix.[domain]"

## 5. Work and education section
- **Work:** Founder · Tank Mix Newsletter · [start date]
- **Description (under work):** Free weekly covering the US ag drone spray industry. Rates, regs, gear, and what's actually working.

## 6. About > Detail About You
Longer-form. This is what shows when someone clicks "See more" on your profile.

> Tank Mix is a free weekly newsletter for US ag drone spray operators and farmer-operators. Every Thursday I cover what's moving in the industry: current rates by region, FAA and state license changes, gear comparisons, real job stories from operators in the field. No fluff, no ads dressed up as articles. If you fly an Agras, a Hylio, or are thinking about adding a drone to your operation, you'll find it useful.
> 
> Free signup: tankmix.[domain]
> 
> Reach me: eugen@agdronedirectory.com

## 7. Featured link
Facebook lets you add ONE clickable link in the intro section. Use this:

> **tankmix.[domain]**

Set the link preview image to your Tank Mix logo or the same cover-photo style asset. If Facebook pulls a generic preview, manually upload a custom one.

## 8. Pinned post (the most important piece)
When someone visits your profile, the pinned post is the top thing they see. Write it once, pin it, leave it pinned for 90 days. Update once a quarter.

Copy:

> If you fly an ag drone for hire, or you're thinking about it, here's what I do.
>
> Every Thursday I send Tank Mix. It's a free weekly that covers the US ag drone spray industry the way you actually need to read about it. Real $/acre rates by region. What changed with the FAA and your state license. Honest gear takes (T40 vs T50 vs Hylio, no vendor BS). Real stories from operators about what's working and what isn't.
>
> No sponsors making me say nice things about gear I haven't flown. No fluff. About 5 minutes to read.
>
> 1,000+ operators and farmer-operators on the list right now. Sign up free: tankmix.[domain]
>
> If you've got a question or a story I should cover, my DMs are open.

Add an image: a single screenshot of a recent Tank Mix issue (your best-looking one), or the cover-photo style asset.

## 9. Photo timeline strategy (the next 30 days)
An empty profile looks suspicious. People click your name and want to see real activity. Build it gradually:

Week 1: Post the pinned post above. That's the only post.

Week 2: Add 1 more post. A photo of a drone in a field with a one-line caption like "Indiana operator working tar spot fungicide passes this week. The fields a ground rig can't get into." Use an image from your Spray Drone Society pack.

Week 3: Add 1 more. A screenshot of a Tank Mix issue with a one-line context like "This week's edition covered [topic]. If you found it useful, share it with a buddy."

Week 4: Add 1 more. A repost of an interesting operator story or industry news with your one-line take.

That's 4 posts in 30 days. Enough to look real, not so many you become a content-mill account that pushes Facebook to penalize your reach.

## 10. Final checklist before you go live
- [ ] Display name set with the · Tank Mix suffix
- [ ] Profile photo uploaded (outdoor, headshot, not a drone photo)
- [ ] Cover photo uploaded with text overlay
- [ ] Bio set to one of the two intro lines
- [ ] Work set to "Founder · Tank Mix Newsletter"
- [ ] About > Detail About You filled in
- [ ] Featured link added (tankmix.[domain])
- [ ] Pinned post published and pinned to top
- [ ] Privacy: profile set to "Public" for posts (otherwise operators can't see your content when they click through)
- [ ] Friend list privacy: set to "Only Me" (you don't want operators seeing your personal friends list)
- [ ] Photos privacy: set to "Public" for the photos you want them to see, "Only Me" for personal/family

## 11. What NOT to put on the profile
- Your political opinions
- Personal photos of family, kids, vacations (set those to "Only Me" or "Friends only")
- Anything that contradicts the operator-friendly, neutral, plain-spoken voice you'll be using in comments
- A second business that competes for attention (if you also run Local Ag Drones agency, keep that off this profile or behind a separate identifier)
- Followers/friends counts that look suspicious (under 50 friends looks new; over 5,000 looks bot)

That's the whole setup. About 90 minutes of work to do right, then it runs on autopilot.
