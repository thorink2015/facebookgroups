This is for us to refine together. It covers three things you asked: what else to offer beyond the newsletter, how to present the kit so they love it, and how to handle the ABZ reply.

## 1. The honest read on your campaign data

You have four sends. Here is what helps you and what hurts you.

**Your power number is the open rate: 51 to 54 percent.** Industry average is 20 to 30. Lead with this everywhere. It is real, defensible and impressive to anyone who buys ads.

**Your clicks need framing, not hiding.** Sentera pulled 5 clicks. On its own that looks thin. But the June 1 feature issue pulled 28 clicks on 847 delivered. So the true story is: this list clicks hard when the offer is strong, and it is a small targeted list where a few right buyers is the point. Present clicks as a range across sends, never the Sentera 5 alone.

**Cut these from any kit:** unsubscribe rate, bounce rate, the subscriber cap (453/1,000). None of it helps a buyer decide. It only invites doubt.

**One caution:** the 847-delivered issue went wider than your core operator list. Be ready to explain that if asked. Do not imply 847 are all verified operators. The verified operator count is 600+, and that is the number that sells.

## 3. How to present so they love it

Buyers say yes when the kit feels like proof, not a pitch. Rules:

1. **Lead with the audience, not the price.** First thing they see: 600+ verified operators they cannot reach elsewhere. Price comes after value.
2. **One big number per section.** Open rate in performance. Operator count in audience. Do not crowd.
3. **Show, do not claim.** The real screenshots of 51 percent opens beat any adjective. Offer to share a screenshot if they want.
4. **Frame the small list as the feature.** "Small on purpose. Every reader is a buyer. Zero waste." Never apologize for size.
5. **Tailor the proof to who they are.** ABZ is a drone maker, so emphasize operators actively buying and comparing drones, and category exclusivity that locks competitors out.
6. **End with one clear next step.** Pick an option, get an agreement and payment link, live in two weeks. No maze of choices.
7. **Keep your voice.** Plain, warm, short. The kit should sound like you, not a corporate media company.

Viktoria asked for four things: audience breakdown, recent performance, options and pricing, and past results. The media kit doc answers all four in that order. Send it with a short warm note, not a long one.

Point her at the $1,150 blast as the simple way to test, and mention the Founding Partner as the bigger play if they want to own the drone category.
