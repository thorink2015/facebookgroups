# Numbers Logic Cheat Sheet

Keep your story straight across every email, kit and call. Use these exact framings.

## The two numbers, never mixed
- **Directory: 600+ verified operators.** Profiles farmers search. The reach asset.
- **Newsletter: 450+ subscribers, growing weekly.** The engaged channel, 50%+ opens.

Funnel story to tell: "600 operators are listed, 450 subscribe to the weekly, and more convert every week as they claim their listing."

## What to show as performance
Show open rate first. It is your strongest, most defensible number.
- Jun 11 issue: 334 reached, 54.19% open
- Jun 4 issue (the John Deere placement): 380 reached, 51.84% open

Two sends, similar size, both above 50%. One clean story.

## What to cut, and why
- **The 847 send: cut it.** It went wider than your real list, had your worst open rate (40%), and breaks the ~450 story. Never show or cite it.
- **Unsubscribe rate, bounce rate, the 453/1,000 cap: cut.** None of it helps a buyer decide.

## Clicks: match the buyer
- Your real click data without the 847 is small (2 to 5 per send).
- Viktoria is Head of PR, so she buys reach and visibility, not clicks. Lead with opens and audience quality. Do not stress clicks.
- For a performance-minded buyer later, run a send with one strong CTA and use that fresh click number.

## The new 450 send (do this)
- Send to the full active list.
- One clear link and call to action so clicks are real and countable.
- Goal: a fresh, consistent data point at full list size that closes the gap between "450 subscribers" and "380 delivered."
- If it opens near 50% with a solid click, add it to the kit as your newest proof.

## If a buyer asks the hard question
"You said 450 subscribers but 380 delivered?"
Answer: "450+ subscribe and the list grows weekly. Each send goes to the active, verified operators after beehiiv cleans inactive addresses. The June 4 placement reached 380, and the list is larger now."

Honest, simple, done.
