## 1. Executive Summary

The Tank Mix Facebook strategy across 426 entities sorts into 56 A-tier groups (post regularly), 151 B-tier (test or lurk), and 219 C-tier (skip).

The strongest conversion plays are: (a) the agronomist/CCA referral channel (one CCA equals thousands of acres of spray decisions), (b) wedding venues as B2B mosquito/grounds service buyers, (c) specialty crops with real spray pain (cotton, hops, pecans, rice, Christmas trees, sorghum), (d) the 5 brand sprayer groups (Apache, Case IH Patriot, Hagie, JD sprayer, RoGator), and (e) demographic leadership audiences (Women in Ag, beginning farmers, veterans).

The deadweight: antique tractor, cattle BST, international, hobbyist drone, indoor growers, and anything tagged FPV or vintage tractor collector. About half the 426 are skips.

The cross-cutting market context: total US drone-sprayed acreage hit 16.4 million acres in 2025 per the American Spray Drone Coalition, a 58.7 percent jump year-over-year. The national custom rate fell to about $13 per acre (down from $21 in 2024). The DJI Agras was added to the FCC Covered List on December 23, 2025. Canada has no in-crop pesticide drone labels yet as of October 2025. FAA-registered ag drones jumped from about 1,000 in January 2024 to about 5,500 by mid-2025.

## 2. Top 5 Strategic Insights

**1. Teach the boring stuff.** Compliance and economics in plain language build more trust than spray-porn videos. The operator who keeps people legal and profitable wins long-term.

**2. The CCA referral channel beats every direct audience.** Per a peer-reviewed study, 80% of farmers consult a crop adviser before spraying and 25-30% rely on that adviser entirely. Four trusted CCAs equals thousands of acres of spray decisions without you posting to a single farmer.

**3. The best audiences nobody else is targeting.** Specialty growers (pecans, hops, alfalfa, organic orchards, Christmas trees) have real spray pain and few options. Wedding venues need recurring mosquito/grounds service. Decision-influencers (farm wives, women in ag, agronomists) move the buying decision.

**4. The head-to-head dollar breakdown is the highest-traveling post format.** Specific numbers travel: $30-40K drone vs $500K+ rig. $13/acre custom vs $12.27/acre owned. 980 acres/year break-even. Real numbers cut through every group.

**5. Never headline the newsletter.** Be the most useful voice in the comments. The mention happens only in DMs after someone asks. Everyone else is selling. You are helping. That is the entire differentiator.

## 3. Voice and Copy Rules

NON-NEGOTIABLE for every Tank Mix Facebook post, comment, DM, and pitch:

- No em dashes, no double dashes, no en dashes
- Banned words: leverage, robust, navigate, delve, seamless, crucially, notably, in conclusion, in essence
- Contractions allowed
- Short punchy sentences
- Specific dollar figures over ranges
- Blue-collar plain direct tone, fifth-to-seventh grade reading level
- No comma before "and" in compound sentences
- No AI-detectable phrasing

When in doubt: write the way an experienced operator at a farm show would actually talk.

## 4. Audience Cluster Deep-Dive

### 4.1 Operators
**Maps to CSV audience_archetype: Operators**
**Categories covered:** Spray Drone Operator, Custom Applicator, Ag Pilot/Mentorship
**Approx group count:** ~30 across batches 1, 2, 3

**Who they are.** Working and aspiring ag spray operators flying DJI Agras, Hylio, XAG, and similar. Mix of full-time custom applicators, farmer-operators flying their own ground, and Part 107 holders considering Part 137. The Custom Applicator subgroup are direct peers (NAAA: UAS in 13% of operations in 2025, up from 5% in 2024).

**Top themes.** Rates per acre, customer acquisition, Part 137 timelines, battery and charging, droplet size and drift, parts availability post-FCC list, drone vs ground rig math, off-season work (mosquito, right-of-way).

**Vocabulary.** "GPA", "droplet size", "Part 137", "tank mix", "label rate", "AgDISP", "swath", "acres per hour", "wet field", "wet draw", "first job", "what's everyone charging".

**Post patterns that work.** Rate questions ("what's drone spray going for in your area?"), photo stories (cab/field shots, before/after kill photos), troubleshooting, gear comparisons, off-season job leads.

**Red flags.** Dealer-controlled groups (AG Spray Drones of North America = Agri Spray Drones) are link-hostile. Brand-controlled (XAG groups) require help-only posts. Aerial Applicators group includes manned pilots who may resent drones; never claim drones replace planes. Rate arguments get heated.

**Best post templates.** T1 (rate question), T3 (photo story), T7 (honest gear take), T8 (DJI ban).

**Best keywords.** RATES, REG, GEAR, DJI.

### 4.2 Farmer-Operators
**Maps to CSV audience_archetype: Farmer-operator**
**Categories covered:** Sprayer Brand Owner (self-propelled subset), Cattle Breed (commercial), Off-Brand Tractor (compact subset), Cover Crop/Seeding, Dairy, Regional Ag, Demographic (black farmers, regenerative)
**Approx group count:** ~60 across batches 2, 4, 5

**Who they are.** Farmers who spray their own ground or hire it done. Self-propelled rig owners (Apache, Case IH Patriot, Hagie, JD, RoGator). Compact tractor owners (Kioti, LS, Branson, NH compact) running 3-point boom sprayers on 50-500 acres. Cow-calf and seedstock cattle producers managing pasture brush and weed pressure. Cover crop and no-till farmers.

**Top themes.** Buy-vs-hire math, wet-ground and tall-crop limits of the ground rig, drone as the gap-filler. Pasture brush (mesquite, thistle, prickly pear, huisache). Cover crop into standing corn. Compaction. Carbon. Fly control. Soil health.

**Vocabulary.** "Self-propelled", "3-point", "PTO", "wet holes", "wet draw", "compaction", "ruts", "broadleaf", "noxious", "brush encroachment", "cover crop", "interseeding", "no-till".

**Post patterns that work.** Farmer-ask hybrid hooks ("Farmers, honest question: would you pay $15/acre..."), buy-vs-hire math (University of Missouri G1274 puts farmer-owned cost at $12.27/acre, beats custom hire above 980 acres), photo stories of wet-field gap.

**Red flags.** Cattle breed groups skew BST and treaty; only the discussion-style commercial breed groups (Hereford Cattle, Brangus Beef, Simmental, Midwest Brahman) take spray content. 2,4-D drift onto neighbor crops is touchy in Texas. Aminopyralid (GrazonNext, Milestone) carries through manure and compost. Dairy margins are thin; do not oversell. Regenerative crowd never wants synthetic herbicide talk; cover crop seeding only.

**Best post templates.** T4 (farmer-ask), T3 (photo story), T1 (rate question).

**Best keywords.** TANK, RATES.

### 4.3 Drone-Curious
**Maps to CSV audience_archetype: Drone-curious**
**Categories covered:** Drone Adjacent (FAA Part 107, mapping, regional pilots), Drone Learning, Drone Business, Veteran Farmer
**Approx group count:** ~25 across batches 1, 4, 5

**Who they are.** Part 107 holders looking for higher-paying work, regional commercial drone pilots, veteran farmers considering ag drone as a startup business, blue-collar entrepreneurs scoping rural businesses. Recruiting pool for new ag spray operators.

**Top themes.** Making money with a Part 107, the path to Part 137, the DJI ban impact, low-capital ag entry, veteran funding programs (FSA microloan up to $50K at 5% rate cap for veterans).

**Vocabulary.** "Part 107", "BVLOS", "Remote ID", "missions", "gigs", "rates", "next mission" (veterans), "side income".

**Post patterns that work.** Recruiting framing ("Part 107 inspection gigs pay okay but here's what ag spray actually pays"), regulation decode (Part 137 plain English), startup-business framing ($25-60K drone vs $500K+ rig).

**Red flags.** Veterans spot a sales pitch fast. Veteran nonprofit grants (AgVets) fund nonprofits not individuals; do not point veterans there for startup capital. Some commercial groups are UK-based (skip) or FPV-focused (skip).

**Best post templates.** T2 (reg decode), T6 (job lead), T4 (farmer-ask).

**Best keywords.** REG, HOW, TANK.

### 4.4 Specialty Crop
**Maps to CSV audience_archetype: Specialty**
**Categories covered:** Specialty Crop (new crops batch 5), Hunting/Food Plot, Equine (ranch horse pasture)
**Approx group count:** ~40 across batches 2, 4, 5

**Who they are.** Growers of crops with real spray-coverage problems that drones solve better than ground rigs or planes. Cotton (defoliation, PGR, harvest aid), hops (18ft trellis, mildew), pecans (60-80ft canopy, scab), Christmas trees (hillside Fraser fir, scale), rice (levees, wet fields), sorghum (sugarcane aphid), almonds (hull split NOW), sweet potato (vine-covered late season). Also food plot managers and ranch horse owners with pasture weed pain.

**Top themes per crop.**
- **Cotton:** defoliation timing, Pix/mepiquat, NAWF, harvest aid coverage at 10-15 GPA, drift near pecans/gardens
- **Hops:** powdery and downy mildew, top-canopy coverage at 18ft, rutting
- **Pecans:** scab in upper canopy where airblast can't reach, 10-21 day spray intervals
- **Christmas trees:** elongate hemlock scale on underside of old needles, hillside access, NC State backpack mist-blower fallback
- **Rice:** levees, power lines, fungicide passes; LSU rice specialist Ronnie Levy: "Some people are spraying all their fields with drones."
- **Sorghum:** sugarcane aphid thresholds (50-125/leaf TX, 30-40% plants OK), Sivanto, Transform
- **Almonds:** hull split timing, navel orangeworm, upper-canopy coverage where ground rig leaves light
- **Sweet potato:** vine-covered rows where tractor can't run
- **Food plots:** remote timber plots tractor can't reach, drone seeding at $20/acre + seed, frost seeding, June weed control window
- **Ranch horse pasture:** toxic broadleaf (locoweed, larkspur, hoary alyssum, buttercup, nightshade), residue concerns

**Red flags.** Sophisticated crop-specific audiences. Almond growers do not want oversimplified NOW management. Cannabis growers are a legal minefield, skip. Ginseng diggers are wild-harvest, not farming. Hunters/food plot crowd: avoid baiting/feeding debates (illegal in parts of AL, GA, MS). Horse people lead with horse safety not acres/hour. Drift onto neighbor crops varies by region.

**Best post templates.** T3 (photo story), T7 (honest gear take), T4 (farmer-ask for hunters/food plot).

**Best keywords.** GEAR, TANK, ACRES (food plots).

### 4.5 B2B Service Buyers
**Maps to CSV audience_archetype: B2B service buyer / B2B service partner**
**Categories covered:** Agritourism (wedding venues), Adjacent Pro (mosquito control, pest control)
**Approx group count:** ~5 across batches 4, 5

**Who they are.** Owners of properties who need recurring outdoor pest treatment. Wedding venue operators (rural acreage, event calendar-driven). Mosquito control districts and PCO firms adding outdoor service. Pest control professionals expanding into outdoor mosquito/tick work.

**Top themes.** Recurring event-driven service (wedding venues). Drone larvicide vs truck/aerial (US comparison from Vermont BLSG: $20/acre drone vs $42/acre manned aerial). Reaching wetland and tree-line spots ground equipment cannot. Brand-positioning as the responsible local applicator.

**Wedding venue economics.** Half-acre to one-acre property: $500-1000/treatment. One treatment lasts up to 17 days. Best timing 2-3 days before event. Industry context: full-service grounds packages run $800-$1,600/acre/month, so drone fits as additive premium service.

**Mosquito control compliance.** Mosquito control sprays are "economic poison" under FAA 14 CFR Part 137, requiring Part 137 + Part 107. Government districts often operate under Certificate of Authorization instead. AMCA has an official Drone Program. Real US programs: South Walton (2,500+ acres in 2025), Collier County (PrecisionVision 40X), Minnesota Metropolitan MCD (modified DJI Agras T10/T30).

**Red flags.** Wedding venues often have chemical-free contract requirements. Offer OMRI option as upcharge. Pest control firms are referral channel not direct competition; partnership angle only. Most mosquito districts are public budgets not farm budgets, longer sales cycles.

**Best post templates.** B2B service post (custom format, see execution artifacts), T6 (job lead style).

**Best keywords.** TANK.

### 4.6 Decision-Influencers
**Maps to CSV audience_archetype: Decision-influencer**
**Categories covered:** Adjacent Pro (CCA/agronomist), Farm Women, Demographic (Women in Ag)
**Approx group count:** ~6 across batches 4, 5

**Who they are.** People who influence farm spray decisions without being the operator. Crop advisers (CCAs) write spray recommendations. Farm wives often run the books and approve spend (half also work off-farm; per USDA NASS 2022 Census, 58% of farms have at least one female decision maker, women are 36% of all producers and 40% of beginning farmers). Women in Ag leadership groups are operators in their own right, professional, network-driven.

**Top themes per group.**
- **CCAs:** technical resistance management, prescription writing, application method specs. Established 1992, 13,000+ certified across US/Canada per the American Society of Agronomy.
- **Farm wives:** money and budgets, bookkeeping, "talked him into it" decision wins, isolation during harvest. Live label tension: many call themselves farmers and resent the "wife" label.
- **Women in Ag:** professional resources, cost breakdowns, decision tools, mutual support without pity.

**Post patterns that work.** Technical peer voice (for CCAs). Honest cost-and-decision stories (for farm wives). Practical decision tools and respect (for women in ag).

**Red flags.** Hard pitches kill in farm wife support groups. Label sensitivity is real ("farmer" not "farm wife"). CCAs are technical and skeptical; bring real numbers and university citations.

**Best post templates.** T2 (reg decode for CCAs), T4 (farmer-ask for influence audiences), T7 (honest gear take).

**Best keywords.** TANK (everyone), REG (CCAs).

### 4.7 Editorial / Page Partnerships
**Maps to CSV audience_archetype: Editorial partner**
**Categories covered:** Page (Editorial) from Batch 6 (7 entities)

**Who they are.** Facebook Pages (not Groups). Mechanic is fundamentally different: you cannot post in a Page like a Group. Strategy is editorial outreach (comment on their posts, DM admins, pitch guest content, propose cross-features).

**The 7 Pages.** Ag Drone Canada (Saskatoon dealer, ~599 likes), Drone Spray Canada (Adrian Rivard, CADA board, Ontario), Arizona Farm and Ranch Group (AZ ag advocacy, $23B state ag economy), Georgia Hemp Farmers (5,530 likes, GA hemp + crossover specialty), Grow Organic Apples (Holistic Orchard Network, Michael Phillips legacy, 3,098 likes), Veterans to Farmers (CO nonprofit, low tempo, Buck Adams founded then stepped down), Wisconsin Hemp Co-Op (1,493 likes, post-CBD-crash small audience).

**Key context.** Canada has no in-crop pesticide drone labels yet (PRO2026-01 was in consultation until March 25, 2026). Hemp EPA-labeled pesticides: 49 total, 46 biopesticides, only 1 conventional. So Canadian and hemp pages have narrow content angles.

**Best play.** Cross-feature editorial swaps (Drone Spray Canada is the strongest of the 7 because Adrian is media-savvy and CADA-connected). Veteran-operator profile cross-feature with Veterans to Farmers. AZ Farm & Ranch is editorial only (slow cycle).

**Red flags.** Veterans to Farmers leadership in transition, low tempo. Drone Spray Canada is small (~205 likes). Holistic Orchard founder deceased, admin unclear.

### 4.8 Skip Categories
**Maps to CSV final_tier: C (219 of 426 entities)**

**Skip with no exceptions:** Antique tractor (collectors), Cattle Livestock state BST (transactional), Cattle Breed for-sale/treaty boards, Off-Brand Tractor vintage subsets (Oliver/Massey collectors), International non-US groups (Canada has no drone labels), Commercial Specialty indoor growers (mushroom, microgreens, hydroponics), Auction boards, Equipment Parts BST, Lifestyle homestead listings, Cannabis growers (legal minefield), Ginseng Diggers (wild harvest), all UK groups, all FPV builder groups, Drone Owner Community hobbyist camera-drone (DJI Phantom, Autel EVO).

**Lurk-only and goodwill (do not promote):** Beekeeping groups (10 of 10, BeeCheck angle only), Trail Riding (6 of 6, spray-window goodwill only).

The skip-list isn't a dismissal of those people. It's a recognition that spray-drone content does not belong in those audiences and posting anyway burns your account.

## 6. Partnership Channels (5)

1. **CCA / Agronomist referral.** Highest leverage. One CCA = thousands of acres. Build via Custom Applicators Forum, Agronomy/crop consultant group, state CCA association pages.
2. **Wedding venue B2B service.** Recurring summer revenue $500-1000/visit. Build via Wedding Venue Owners group + direct outreach to local venues.
3. **Mosquito control / vector partnership.** Public-health budgets, longer cycles. Build via Texas Mosquito Control YP, AMCA, county districts.
4. **Veteran farmer recruiting.** Career-path angle, drone as fundable startup. Build via FVC (50,000+ members), Veterans to Farmers Page, ODVA AgriVets.
5. **Drone Spray Canada / Ag Drone Canada editorial swap.** Two-way cross-feature with on-topic operators in a related market.

## 7. Industry Data and $/acre Benchmarks

These numbers are gold. They appear in many of the templates and emails. Source them once and use repeatedly.

**Market size and growth.**
- 16.4 million US acres sprayed by drone in 2025 (American Spray Drone Coalition, ASDC annual survey)
- 58.7% YoY growth in 2025 (from 10.3M acres in 2024)
- 2024: $215M rural revenue from US ag drone spraying (ASDC)
- FAA-registered ag drones: ~1,000 in Jan 2024 to ~5,500 by mid-2025 (Michigan State University, Nov 2025)
- 2024: ~8,950 new spray drones sold. 2025: ~3,711 (59% drop) per ASDC
- DJI controls ~80% of US ag spray drone sales (Michigan State / Science study)

**Rates per acre (2026).**
- $13/acre national average drone spray (ASDC 2025 update; down from $21 in 2024)
- $12.50/acre median (Iowa State 2026 Custom Rate Survey, 47 operators)
- $12.27/acre farmer-owned cost at 1,000 acres (University of Missouri Extension G1274, March 2025, modeling DJI Agras T-40)
- $7.39/acre farmer-owned cost at 4,000 acres (same source)
- $16/acre custom hire benchmark
- 980 acres/year break-even for farmer ownership vs custom hire (Univ Missouri G1274)
- $5/acre profit at current rates per AgDroneDirectory.com (2026 Rates Guide)
- Specialty crops still hold $18-40/acre

**Beck's 2025 Practical Farm Research Corn Fungicide Trial.**
- Drone application at 2 gallons/acre carrier returned $27.26/acre ROI and +12 bushels over the check
- Beat both ground rig and plane on ROI per Venture Ag Drones reporting

**Equipment cost.**
- New ag spray drone: $25,000-60,000 (most operators)
- Hylio AG-272 starts at $56,000 (no kit), about $72,000 with batteries and charger (Apple Farm Service)
- DJI Agras T50: $22,000-28,000 after tariffs ($18,000 pre-tariff base, MSRP per DroneXL)
- Self-propelled ground rig: $150,000-500,000+
- Used DJI Agras T30: $9,500-16,000 (XAGSupply, Controller.com)
- Used DJI Agras T40: $15,900-28,000

**Tariff context (April 2025).**
- 170% total tariff burden on Chinese drones at peak (25% Section 301 + 20% fentanyl + 125% reciprocal) per DSLRPros/DroneXL
- Section 122 surcharge of 10% expires July 24, 2026
- DJI reports projected $1.56B loss in 2026 (DroneXL, 9th Cir appeal brief)

**Mosquito control drone economics.**
- $20/acre drone larvicide vs $42/acre manned aerial (Vermont BLSG district)
- Drone larvicide adoption: South Walton 2,500+ acres in 2025, Collier County 1,000+ acres, MN Metropolitan MCD modified Agras T10/T30

**Cover crop drone seeding.**
- ~$20/acre drone seeding + cost of seed
- 15-20 lb/acre seeding rate sweet spot for small seed (rye, clover)
- Drone-seeded biomass beat drilled biomass by 3x in one AAFC trial (Oscar Molina)

**Veteran funding.**
- FSA Microloan up to $50,000, interest rate capped at 5% for veterans (USDA FSA, 2024)
- Operating + ownership microloans combined up to $100,000
- Farmer Veteran Coalition: 50,000+ members nationwide (Aug 2024 milestone)

**Regulatory anchors.**
- DJI added to FCC Covered List Dec 23, 2025
- DJI Agras T55 (FCC ID SS3-T55A2510) approved Dec 19, 2025 (3 days before Covered List)
- Canada: 5 pesticide products approved for drone application as of Oct 13, 2025, ALL non-crop (mosquito larvicide, ROW, industrial)
- Canada PRO2026-01 consultation closed March 25, 2026
- Hemp pesticides: 49 EPA-approved labels, 46 biopesticides, 1 conventional

**Manned aviation context (NAAA 2025).**
- Fixed-wing in 88% of operations
- Helicopters in 16%
- UAS in 13% of operations (up from 5% in 2024, 160% increase YoY)
