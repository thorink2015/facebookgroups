## Where drone wins
- Field is wet or rutted, ground rig can't get in
- Crop is tall (corn past V12, silage, sorghum, hops at 18 feet)
- Field is small, odd-shaped, hillside, or near sensitive areas a plane won't fly
- Spot spray instead of broadcast (brush, thistle, mesquite, johnsongrass)
- Cover crop into standing corn or beans before harvest
- Drift-sensitive neighbors (precise, low-drift droplet, no wheel tracks)

## Where ground rig or plane wins
- Large open dry fields, full season, where rig speed and capacity dominate
- Pre-emerge herbicide on bare ground, high carrier rate
- Anything requiring 15-plus gallons per acre carrier

## Application specs
- Carrier rate: 2 gallons per acre standard, up to 3 for dense canopy
- Droplet size: 200 to 250 microns, never above 300
- Flight: 10 to 15 feet above canopy, under 22 mph, wind 5 to 10 mph
- Coverage: top-down deposition, hits upper and middle canopy where airblast misses
- Productivity: 300 to 500 acres per day per drone
