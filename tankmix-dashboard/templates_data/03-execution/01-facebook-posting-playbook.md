# Tank Mix — Facebook Group Posting Playbook v1

The plan to turn 426 groups into Tank Mix subscribers without burning your account or your time.

---

## 1. The frame

You are not selling a newsletter. You are dropping useful posts that prove you know the trade, and the newsletter becomes the obvious next step for anyone who wants more. Hard-sell posts get deleted. Useful posts get pinned by admins and shared by members.

**One rule above all:** never put the subscribe link in the post body. Facebook suppresses external links. Put the link in the first comment OR drive a comment-keyword reply OR send people to your profile bio. Every template below follows this.

---

## 2. Four audience buckets (forget the 6-tier CSV when posting)

| **B. Farmer-operators** | Farmer/regional ag/cattle/tractor/sprayer brand groups | Folksy, savings angle | T1, T3, T4, T5 |

## 3. The 8 post templates

Each one is copy-paste ready. Brackets are the only thing you swap. Each template has a soft CTA that does not break group rules.

### T1. The Rate Question (highest engagement)
Best for: Operators, Farmer-operators, Regional ag

```
Question for the room. What's drone spray going for in your area this year?

Pulled the latest numbers from this week and the regional spread is wider than I expected:
- Corn Belt: $12 to $18
- Delta: $15 to $21
- California: $18 to $35
- Mountain West: $10 to $22

What are you guys actually charging or actually paying? Curious what's holding above $15 and what's dropping.

(I track this every Thursday in a free weekly. Reply "rates" and I'll send the full breakdown.)
```

Why it works: rates are the #1 most-searched topic in the niche. People always answer. Comments do the work.

## 4. The comment-keyword trick (your secret weapon)

You cannot put a subscribe link in the post body without Facebook killing your reach. So you do this instead:

1. Post the template
2. At the bottom say "Comment '[keyword]' and I'll send you the breakdown"
3. When someone comments, you reply with the Tank Mix link AND DM them the link
4. The comment counts as engagement, boosting the post
5. The DM is direct and personal

This is the legit version of the "ManyChat" trick. You do it manually. Five minutes a day.

**Keywords to rotate:** rates, reg, gear, DJI, acres, tank, yes, in, send.

Track which keywords pull best. After 2 weeks you'll know your top 3.

## 5. Image strategy (use what you already have, add 3 simple types)

You already have the Spray Drone Society image prompt pack. For Facebook posts, you need fewer hero shots and more "post-native" images.

**Build these 4 image types and reuse forever:**

| Type | What it is | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Hero photo** | The golden-hour T50 + operator shots from your existing prompt pack | T3, T5, T6, T8 |
| **Rate card screenshot** | A simple table of regional $/acre numbers, designed in Canva, looks like a magazine pull quote | T1 |
| **Plain text quote card** | One line from a Tank Mix issue on a bone-white background, Tank Mix wordmark bottom right | T2, T4, T8 |
| **Newsletter screenshot** | Crop of a Tank Mix issue showing one segment (The Window, The Going Rate, etc), with the issue date visible | T7, profile pinned post |

**Image format rule:** square (1080x1080) for in-feed, vertical (1080x1350) for high-engagement posts. Facebook still favors square in groups. Always include the Tank Mix wordmark small in one corner.

The rate card and quote card you build once each in Canva, then duplicate weekly with new numbers. 10 minutes per week.

## 6. The A/B test (test 2 things, never 5)

Do not A/B test entire posts. You'll learn nothing.

Test ONLY these two variables:

1. **The first 8 words of the hook.** Facebook shows the first 1.5 lines before the "see more" cut. Your job is to earn the click on "see more."
2. **The image.** Hero photo vs quote card vs rate card.

Hold these constant: the body, the CTA, the keyword.

**How:** post the same template to 2 similar groups within the same audience bucket on the same day, with one variable changed. Wait 48 hours. Compare comments + reactions. Keep the winner. Discard the loser. Move on.

After 4 weeks of this you'll have 3 to 4 templates that print every time.

## 7. The 2-week kickoff cadence

Don't try to post in 426 groups. That's how you get banned. Start narrow.

**Week 1: Lurk + Learn**
- Join 30 groups from your Tier 1 + Tier 2 + a few Tier 3 farmer groups
- Read the pinned rules in every one
- Comment helpfully on 3 to 5 posts per day, no links, no pitch
- Identify the 10 groups where activity is real and admins are not hostile
- DM admins of your top 5 groups: "I write a free weekly for ag drone operators. Mind if I share useful content here? Happy to follow your rules."

**Week 2: Start posting**
- Post in 3 groups per day, rotating templates
- Use T1 (Rate Question) and T3 (Photo Story) as your openers, they get the highest engagement and lowest takedown risk
- Track in a spreadsheet: group name, template used, image used, comments, reactions, keyword opt-ins
- Reply to every comment within 2 hours

**Week 3 onward:** scale to 5 groups per day, rotate all 8 templates, expand to Tier 3 and Tier 4 groups, drop any template that's not converting.

## 8. Compliance rules (the no-go list)

1. Never post from a brand page. Use your personal profile with "Founder of Tank Mix" in the bio.
2. Never post the subscribe link in the post body. First comment or DM only.
3. Never copy-paste the exact same post in 5 groups same day. Facebook flags it. Rewrite the hook each time.
4. Never argue in comments. If someone calls you out, thank them and move on.
5. Never post in a group you've been in less than 7 days. New-member posts get auto-removed by most admins.
6. Never tag the newsletter as "free trial" or "sign up now." Tank Mix is "the weekly" or "the free weekly."
7. Admin DMs first. Some of these admins are dealers (Agri Spray Drones, nuWay Ag, Southern Drone OPS). Offer them a Tank Mix sponsor mention in exchange for posting rights. That turns enemies into allies.

You have 575 Tank Mix subscribers today. Here's what to expect from Facebook:

That gets you from 575 to 1,000 (the sponsor-revenue threshold) in 4 to 6 weeks. Not 6 months.
