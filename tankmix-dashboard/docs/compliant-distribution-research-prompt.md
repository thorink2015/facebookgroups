# Research prompt: compliant, scalable distribution for the Tank Mix newsletter

Paste the prompt below into a fresh Claude conversation (ideally with the
**deep-research** skill, or Claude with web search). It's written to get a practical,
sourced playbook for reaching spray-drone operators **at scale without breaking
Facebook's rules or risking your account** — the legitimate version of what you
wanted the bot to do.

---

> I run a free newsletter called **Tank Mix** for the agricultural **spray-drone /
> ag-aviation** niche (operators, farmer-operators, drone-curious folks, and
> specialty growers). I want to grow subscribers by getting in front of these
> communities **at scale**, but **entirely within platform rules** — no automation
> that violates terms, no bot-detection evasion, no risk to my personal account.
>
> Give me a prioritized, **2026-current** distribution playbook. For each tactic
> include: what it is, realistic effort/cost, expected payoff, the main risk, and a
> concrete first step. Cite sources and flag anything that's an estimate.
>
> Cover, at minimum:
> 1. **Facebook, the compliant way.** What can I actually do within terms in 2026?
>    Posting from a **Page** vs a personal profile; creating/admin-ing **my own
>    group**; **Meta Business Suite** scheduling (what's allowed to schedule, to
>    Pages and/or groups); boosting/ads targeting ag/drone interests; partnering
>    with existing group admins (cross-posts, pinned posts, takeovers); the current
>    status of any official **Groups API** access for posting. Be specific about
>    what is permitted vs against terms.
> 2. **Other communities** where this audience lives: relevant **subreddits**,
>    niche **forums**, **Discord/Slack** servers, **LinkedIn** groups, YouTube,
>    and industry directories — with the etiquette/self-promo rules for each.
> 3. **Content-led growth**: lead magnets, SEO/long-tail, short-form video, a
>    referral loop, and repurposing one newsletter issue across channels.
> 4. **Partnerships**: drone dealers, chemical/seed reps, ag influencers,
>    associations, and trade publications — how to approach them.
> 5. **What to avoid**: tactics that get accounts/Pages limited or banned, and the
>    realistic consequences.
>
> Finish with a **30-day action plan** I can execute solo, ordered by
> payoff-per-hour, that could plausibly add the most subscribers without endangering
> my main account.

---
